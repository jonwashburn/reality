\begin{abstract}
We present a first\textendash principles framework in which the laws and constants of physics emerge as outputs of a single mechanised axiom, rather than as a model fitted to data. From the impossibility of self\textendash referential non\textendash existence (Meta\textendash Principle), we derive: (i) a positive, double\textendash entry ledger; (ii) a unique convex symmetric cost functional \(J(x)=\tfrac12(x+x^{-1})-1\) (with strict convexity and fixed local scale); (iii) a self\textendash similar scaling fixed point \(\varphi\) (the golden ratio); and (iv) in \(D=3\), a minimal \(2^{D}\)–tick clock (eight\textendash beat) that anchors discretised dynamics. These results are formalised as eight core theorems (T1–T8), machine\textendash verified in Lean~4. They yield a proof layer of dimensionless identities and fixed pipelines with no new parameters at the phenomenology layer. Dimensionless predictions are mapped to SI via two audited routes and a single inequality. The resulting coverage includes a ladder spectrum for masses \(m=B\,E_{\rm coh}\,\varphi^{\,r+f}\), a path\textendash measure derivation of the Born rule and Bose/Fermi statistics, and an information\textendash limited gravity kernel consistent with galaxy rotation and linear growth; e.g., \(\alpha^{-1}\approx 137.036\) (digits via a deterministic pipeline). Policy: \(c\) and \(h\) (hence \(\hbar\)) are SI\,–\,defined and \(G\) is empirically anchored; we do not claim to predict their SI numbers. Strict theorems are distinguished from report\,–\,level bundles (e.g., \texttt{PrimeClosure}, \texttt{UltimateClosure}). Artifacts and procedures are documented in a consolidated Lean core and a classical bridge specification (units quotient and audit gates for \(\{c,\hbar,G,\alpha\}\)).%
This is a working theory in the scientific sense—a structured explanatory framework with testable implications—not a proved equivalence to all of physics.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

This paper presents a single measurement instrument for physics built from one axiom and no tunable parameters. The instrument formalizes a \emph{recognition event} (what it means for structure to be the same or different) and records such events on a minimal \emph{ledger} (see glossary Table~\ref{tab:glossary}). From this setup we derive a unique, dimensionless, symmetric cost \(J\) (convex, parity-balanced, linear under coarse-graining, and antipodal under inversion) with the normalization \(J''(1)=1\). The derivation is internal and does not assume any physical constants.

Three core results follow from the ledger alone. First, the unique interior fixed point of the symmetry-normalized cost is the golden ratio \(\varphi\), which acts as the canonical scaling pivot for dimensionless balances. Second, minimal link and voxel combinatorics force a three-dimensional spatial scaffold: the admissible identification rules that preserve recognition without contradiction close only in three dimensions. Third, the minimal consistent update clock on the primitive cell is an eight-tick cycle: the smallest schedule that respects the ledger's symmetry and causality constraints has period \(2^3\).

To connect the instrument to observed physics we add a single \emph{bridge} statement: dimensioned quantities used in experiments must be quotients of ledger-certified dimensionless balances that pass explicit audit inequalities. Under this bridge, the familiar constants \(\{c,\hbar,G,\alpha\}\) appear as the scale choices that close those audits. The result is a parameter-free mapping from theorems about \(J\), \(\varphi\), and the eight-tick/3D scaffold to concrete predictions in SI units.

Several standard structures then come out of the same calculus. Quantum statistics arise from symmetrized path weights on the ledger; the Born rule is the unique probability assignment compatible with the cost. Gravity appears as an information-limited kernel on recognition flows, which reduces to the tested weak-field and lensing behaviors in the appropriate limits while exposing measurable deviations at small scales. A compact spectral constructor,
\begin{equation}
  m \;=\; B\,E_{\mathrm{coh}}\,\varphi^{\,r+f},
  \label{eq:mass-law}
\end{equation}
organizes particle families at a common renormalization scale, with fixed family ratios and coherence energy \(E_{\mathrm{coh}}\) determined by the ledger rather than by fit parameters.

\noindent\emph{Scope note.} Only the first two items above are strict theorems inside the ledger calculus; the 3D forcing relies on the link-penalty mechanism together with Jordan/ambient-isotopy arguments (Appendix~\ref{app:dimension-link}).

The paper makes three contributions. (i) A formal core that proves uniqueness of the cost \(J\), identifies \(\varphi\) as the fixed point, forces three-space, and derives the eight-tick clock. (ii) A precise bridge specification that turns those dimensionless statements into SI-level predictions through auditable gates; one fully worked example is carried out end-to-end. (iii) A set of quantitative tests and falsifiers that stress the framework in independent regimes: digits for \(\alpha^{-1}\) under a stated budget, renormalization-stable family-ratio checks in the mass constructor, laboratory gravity nulls at short distances, cosmological growth and background kernels, and targeted electroweak anomalies. Each claim is paired with an error budget and a concrete failure mode.

We also include a dedicated benchmarks section (§15) that reports compact, auditable comparisons: digits for \(\alpha^{-1}\) versus CODATA and anchor-level PDG checks (transported to the common anchor), with artifact paths and checksums.

\noindent\textbf{Claims vs. Anchors.} We treat \(c\) and \(h\) (hence \(\hbar\)) as SI\,–\,defined yardsticks and \(G\) as empirically anchored; we do not claim to predict their SI values. What we do claim are dimensionless identities and fixed pipelines that yield numbers without tunable parameters (e.g., digits of \(\alpha\), family ratios \(m_i/m_j=\varphi^{\Delta r}\) at \(\mu_\star\), the ILG kernel form and its GR limit). Every mapping from dimensionless statements to SI is audited: the two routes must pass a single inequality, datasets and toolchains are pinned (commit and checksums), and a one\,–\,command rebuild regenerates all reported numbers. No per\,–\,species tuning is allowed; sector yardsticks and residues are fixed once and stress\,–\,tested by ablations.

Scope and limits are explicit. What is called a theorem is proved inside the ledger calculus without physical inputs. What is called a prediction depends only on the bridge audits, which are stated as inequalities that can fail in experiment. The aim is not to rebrand known fits, but to show that one instrument—one ledger, one cost, one bridge—accounts for a wide span of phenomena without free parameters and invites precise attempts to break it.

The presentation is simple on purpose. First we develop the axioms and derive the core results (\(J\), \(\varphi\), three-space, eight-tick). Next we state the bridge and work through a complete example from a dimensionless identity to an SI-level number with its audit. We then consolidate predictions and falsifiers across domains. Appendices collect proof details, constructor checks, and reproducibility instructions.

\section{Overview: Reality as a Computational Framework}
\label{sec:overview}

\paragraph{What Recognition Science is.}
Recognition Science (RS) is a first‑principles theory that treats reality as a minimal, logically consistent information‑processing framework. From a single tautology—\emph{``nothing cannot recognize itself''} (the Meta‑Principle)—RS \emph{derives} the unique computational architecture whose outputs are the laws and constants of physics. There are no adjustable parameters at the derivation layer; all physical structure is a consequence of logical necessity.

\paragraph{Why computation?}
Every physical measurement is fundamentally an \emph{information‑processing event}: distinguishing one state from another requires recording, comparing, and updating. If we demand that this process be (i)~logically consistent, (ii)~countable (no infinite precision), and (iii)~cost‑minimal, the mathematical structure is uniquely determined. Reality emerges as the \emph{only} zero‑parameter algorithm compatible with these constraints.

\paragraph{The computational substrate.}
To track recognition events, nature implements a \emph{double‑entry ledger}—a bookkeeping system where every state‑change creates balanced debit/credit pairs. This ledger must be:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Positive:} No negative existence (entries $>0$)
  \item \textbf{Discrete:} Countable ticks and quantized units ($\delta\cong\mathbb{Z}$)
  \item \textbf{Minimal:} Lowest cost functional, forced to be $J(x)=\tfrac{1}{2}(x+x^{-1})-1$
\end{itemize}

\begin{definition}[Channel count and composition prefactor]
\label{def:channel-count}
At the matching scale \(\mu_\star\), the \emph{channel count} \(n_c\) of a species is the number of independent recognition channels: disjoint, simultaneously postable double\,–\,entry paths that must be tracked to conserve the word\,–\,charge during binding/transport. For elementary species, \(n_c=1\). For composites, \(n_c\) equals the number of valence channels required by the constructor (e.g., baryons have three).

The sector/composition prefactor is fixed by the \emph{doubling per channel} rule
\[
  B \;=\; 2^{\,n_c},
\]
which is part of the RS\(\to\)Classical bridge specification and audited in Table~\ref{tab:sector_yardsticks}. This is not a fit: \(n_c\) is determined by the constructor and sector policy, and \(B\) is thereby fixed (no per\,–\,species tuning).
\end{definition}
These are not design choices—they are \emph{logically forced} by the Meta‑Principle. A computer scientist would recognize this as the minimal persistent data structure for tracking state changes under conservation laws.

\paragraph{What the computation determines (with zero free parameters).}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{The clock speed: 8 ticks.} A minimal sweep of a cubic voxel ($2^{3}{=}8$ vertices) defines the fundamental period. This is the smallest cycle that visits every spatial vertex exactly once.
  
  \item \textbf{The scaling constant: golden ratio.} Self‑similarity under minimal cost forces $\varphi{=}(1{+}\sqrt{5})/2$, the unique solution to $\varphi^{2}{=}\varphi{+}1$ with $k{=}1$ (minimal postings per tick).
  
  \item \textbf{The spatial dimension: $D{=}3$.} Only 3D supports irreducible linking (``tying a knot''). 2D cannot link; 4D+ allows all knots to unravel, lowering ledger cost and violating minimality.
  
  \item \textbf{Physical constants from $\varphi$.} The coherence quantum $E_{\rm coh}{=}\varphi^{-5}$ (five degrees of freedom: 3 space, 1 time, 1 dual‑balance). Masses scale as $m{=}B\,E_{\rm coh}\,\varphi^{r+f}$ where $r$ is an integer rung from the constructor and $f$ a renormalization residue. The fine‑structure constant $\alpha$ follows from geometric seed, gap series, and curvature closure.
  
  \item \textbf{Quantum rules from path counting.} The Born rule $P{=}|\psi|^{2}$ emerges from exponential path weighting ($e^{-C[\gamma]}$). Bose/Fermi statistics follow from permutation symmetry (one‑dimensional irreps minimize expected cost).

\end{itemize}

\noindent\emph{Measurement/bridge scope (no-medium-knobs).} Dimensionless \emph{bridge displays} (after the units quotient) cannot depend on medium parameters (e.g., refractive index, diffusion constants, Q-factors). Such parameters may appear in the \emph{measurement layer} as noise or efficiency modifiers, but they cannot alter the RS invariants or the dimensionless identities used at the bridge. \section{The Canonical Cost and Scaling (central equalities)}

\subsection{Unique convex symmetric cost}\label{subsec:unique-cost}

\paragraph{Result.}
\[
  \boxed{\,J(x)=\tfrac12\!\left(x+\dfrac{1}{x}\right)-1\;,\qquad x\in\mathbb R_{>0}\,}
\]

\begin{theorem}[Convex symmetric cost: uniqueness on $\mathbb R_{>0}$]\label{thm:cost-uniqueness}
Let $J:\mathbb R_{>0}\to\mathbb R_{\ge0}$ satisfy the SALP assumptions (Symmetry, Analyticity, Ledger finiteness, Positivity/normalization):
\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{(S) Symmetry (dual-balance):} $J(x)=J(x^{-1})$ for all $x>0$;
  \item \textbf{(A) Analyticity off the origin:} $J$ extends analytically to $\mathbb C\setminus\{0\}$;
  \item \textbf{(L) Ledger finiteness bound:} $\exists K>0$ with $J(x)\le K\,(x+1/x)$ for all $x>0$;
  \item \textbf{(P) Normalization/positivity:} $J(1)=0$ is the unique minimum, $J(x)>0$ for $x\neq 1$;
  \item \textbf{Local scale:} $J''(1)=1$.
\end{enumerate}
Then $J(x)=\tfrac12(x+1/x)-1$ on $\mathbb R_{>0}$ (in particular, $J$ is strictly convex on $\mathbb R_{>0}$). Full derivations including tail exclusions and alternative forms appear in Appendix~\ref{app:cost-uniqueness}.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}[Sketch]
By symmetry and analyticity away from the origin, $J$ admits a symmetric Laurent expansion on $\mathbb C\setminus\{0\}$; restricting to $\mathbb R_{>0}$ one may write
\[
  J(x)\;=\;c_0\;+\;\sum_{n\ge1} c_n\,\bigl(x^n+x^{-n}\bigr).
\]
If any $c_{n_{\max}}\ne 0$ with $n_{\max}\ge2$, then $J(x)/(x+1/x)\sim c_{n_{\max}}\,x^{\,n_{\max}-1}\to\infty$ as $x\to\infty$, contradicting the finiteness bound. Hence only the $n=1$ term survives, so $J(x)=c_0+c_1(x+1/x)$. From $J(1)=0$ we get $c_0=-2c_1$, and the local scale $J''(1)=1$ yields $2c_1=1$, i.e. $c_1=\tfrac12$. Therefore $J(x)=\tfrac12(x+1/x)-1$. In particular, $J''(x)=x^{-3}>0$ for $x>0$, so $J$ is strictly convex on $\mathbb R_{>0}$.
\end{proof}

\noindent\emph{Machine status and bridge.} The uniqueness statement is mechanised as theorem~T5 in the RS proof layer and mapped to the classical stationary-action/Dirichlet bridge (details and status table, see \cite{RS_Lean_Artifact,RS_Bridge_Spec}). Full proof details are collected in the proofs appendix; the main text retains concise statements to avoid duplication.

\paragraph{Uses.}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Minimal action / Dirichlet bridge.} Setting $y=\ln x$ yields $J(e^{y})=\cosh y-1$, so variational stationarity coincides locally with harmonicity in $y$ (Dirichlet energy).
  \item \textbf{Quadratic vicinity (metric).} For $x=1+\varepsilon$, $J(1+\varepsilon)=\tfrac12\varepsilon^{2}+O(\varepsilon^{3})$, fixing the canonical small-imbalance metric.
  \item \textbf{Legendre dual.} In the log-variable $y$, the convex conjugate of $\cosh y-1$ is
  \begin{equation}\label{eq:legendre-dual}
     J^{\!*}(p)=\sup_{y}\{py-(\cosh y-1)\}=p\,\operatorname{arsinh}p-\sqrt{1+p^{2}}+1,
  \end{equation}
  furnishing the Hamiltonian/co-state form used later for flow laws and bounds.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Self-similar fixed point and the golden ratio}\label{subsec:phi-fixed-point}

\paragraph{Result.}
Self-similarity with integer $k$-ary splitting enforces the recurrence
\[
  x_{n+1}=1+\frac{k}{x_n},
\]
whose fixed point solves $x=1+k/x \;\Longleftrightarrow\; x^{2}-x-k=0$.  
Countability and global cost minimisation select $k=1$, hence
\[
  \boxed{\,x=\varphi=\dfrac{1+\sqrt5}{2}\,}.
\]

\begin{lemma}[Countability $\Rightarrow$ integer $k$]\label{lem:int-k}
Interpreting $k/x_n$ as $k$ \emph{indivisible} sub-recognitions posted in one tick on a discrete ledger forces $k\in\mathbb N$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Each ledger post is one unit action; fractions would violate the atomic tick and unit granularity (T2, see \cite{RS_Lean_Artifact}).
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Cost monotonicity in $k$]\label{lem:k-monotone}
Let $\Sigma(k):=\sum_{n\ge0}J(x_n(k))$ for $x_{n+1}=1+k/x_n$ with $x_0>1$ and $J$ from Thm.~\ref{thm:cost-uniqueness}. Then $\Sigma(k)$ is strictly increasing in the integer $k\ge1$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
By induction $x_n(k)$ increases with $k$; since $J'(x)>0$ for $x>1$, each summand increases in $k$. More precisely, for the partial sums $\Sigma_N(k):=\sum_{n=0}^{N} J\bigl(x_n(k)\bigr)$ one has $\Sigma_N(k{+}1)>\Sigma_N(k)$ for all integers $k\ge1$ and all $N\ge0$. Passing to the limit $N\to\infty$ preserves monotonicity (including the case of divergence to $+\infty$), so $\Sigma(k)$ is strictly increasing in $k$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Uniqueness of the scaling constant]\label{thm:k-equals-one}
Among admissible integers $k\ge1$, the ledger-optimal choice is $k=1$. The resulting fixed point is the golden ratio $\varphi$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
By Lemma~\ref{lem:int-k}, $k\in\mathbb N$. By Lemma~\ref{lem:k-monotone}, $\Sigma(k)$ is minimised at the smallest admissible $k$, i.e.\ $k=1$. The fixed-point equation is $x=1+1/x$, so $x^{2}-x-1=0$ with positive root $\varphi$.
\end{proof}

\paragraph{Note (exclusion of $k\neq 1$).}

\subsection{Meta‑Principle (MP): ``Nothing cannot recognize itself.''}
\label{subsec:mp}

\begin{definition}[Recognition structure]
\label{def:recognition-structure}
A \emph{recognition structure} is a triple
\(
\mathcal M=\langle U,\emptyset,\triangleright\rangle
\),
where $U$ is a non‑empty set whose elements are \emph{entities}, and $\emptyset\in U$ denotes the designated \emph{"nothing"} entity (a conventional name; in the Lean formalization this is represented by the empty type). The relation
$\triangleright\subseteq U\times U$ is a binary relation read
``$a$ recognizes $b$''.  A \emph{recognition} is an ordered pair
$a\triangleright b$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Meta‑Principle (MP)]
\label{thm:MP}
In any recognition structure, there does not exist a self‑recognition of
nothing:
\[
  \neg\bigl(\emptyset\triangleright\emptyset\bigr).
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Assume \(\emptyset\triangleright\emptyset\). By the intended meaning of
\(\triangleright\) a recognition record requires a witness for the recogniser; in the formal development this corresponds to a value of the empty type, which is impossible. Hence a contradiction. A fully formalised (Lean 4) proof appears in the appendix. \qedhere
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Role of MP]
MP is \emph{the} axiom.  Everything that follows is a theorem (or a short
chain of lemmas) resting on this single statement.
\end{remark}

\subsection{Necessity of a positive double‑entry ledger (existence/uniqueness; \(\delta\)‑unit)}
\label{subsec:ledger-necessity}

\begin{definition}[Ledger]
\label{def:ledger}
Given a recognition structure \(\mathcal M\), a \emph{ledger} is a triple
\(\langle C,\iota,\kappa\rangle\) where $C$ is a totally ordered abelian
group and \(\iota,\kappa:U\to C\) satisfy, for some fixed generator
\(\delta\in C_{>0}\),
\begin{align*}
\text{(L1) Double entry:}&\quad
  \iota(b)-\kappa(a)=\delta\quad\text{for every }a\triangleright b;\\
\text{(L2) Positivity:}&\quad
  \iota(x),\kappa(x)>0\quad\text{for all }x\neq\emptyset;\\
\text{(L3) Conservation:}&\quad
  \sum_{k=1}^{n}\bigl[\iota(a_k)-\kappa(a_{k-1})\bigr]=0
  \text{ for every finite recognition chain }a_0\triangleright\cdots\triangleright a_n.
\end{align*}
\end{definition}

\begin{lemma}[Exclusion of $k$‑ary and modular accounts]
\label{lem:kary-modular}
No $k$‑ary ($k\ge3$) generalisation of double‑entry accounting, and no
modular‑cost ledger $(C,\oplus)$ with finite modulus $m>0$, is compatible
with MP, composability of \(\triangleright\), and finiteness of recognition
chains.  In each case orphan costs or zero‑cost non‑trivial cycles arise.
\end{lemma}

\begin{lemma}[Non‑rescalability of the generator]
\label{lem:delta-fixed}
If \(\delta>0\) satisfies \emph{(L1)}–\emph{(L3)}, there is no order‑preserving
automorphism of $C$ sending \(\delta\mapsto s\delta\) with $s\neq1$.
Any $s\neq 1$ produces an infinite ascending/descending chain of positive
costs, contradicting finiteness.
\end{lemma}

\begin{theorem}[Ledger–Necessity (existence \& uniqueness)]
\label{thm:ledger-necessity}
Every recognition structure satisfying MP admits a positive, double‑entry
ledger in the sense of Definition~\ref{def:ledger}, and any two such
ledgers are order‑isomorphic.  Moreover the subgroup generated by \(\delta\)
is isomorphic to \(\mathbb Z\) (quantised unit increments).
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}[Proof sketch]
\emph{Existence.}  Start from the free abelian group on recognitions
\(\bigoplus_{(a,b)}\mathbb Z\,[a\triangleright b]\) and impose the
anti‑symmetry relations \([a\triangleright b]+[b\triangleright a]=0\).
Define \(\delta:=[a\triangleright b]\) for a fixed seed edge and post
entries by \(\iota(b)-\kappa(a)=\delta\); \emph{(L3)} follows by telescoping.

\emph{Uniqueness.}  The construction is universal: any other positive
double‑entry ledger realises the same relations, hence is obtained by a
unique order‑preserving isomorphism.

\emph{Rigidity of \(\delta\).}  Lemma~\ref{lem:delta-fixed} forbids a global
rescaling; Lemma~\ref{lem:kary-modular} eliminates $k$‑ary and modular
alternatives.  Therefore \(\langle\delta\rangle\cong\mathbb Z\) and the
ledger is unique up to order‑isomorphism.  \qedhere
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Quantisation of postings]
\label{cor:Z-units}
Ledger postings occur in integer multiples of a fixed, positive unit
\(\delta\); there are no fractional or negative elementary recognitions.
\end{corollary}

\subsection{Atomic Tick (no concurrency): exactly one recognition per tick}
\label{subsec:atomic-tick}

\begin{definition}[Atomic Tick]
\label{def:atomic-tick}
At each tick \(t\in\mathbb Z\) exactly one elementary double-entry \((+\delta,-\delta)\) may be posted; concurrency is forbidden at granularity \(\tau_0\).
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Tick schedule]
A \emph{tick} is the minimal temporal interval to post one elementary
double‑entry record $(+\delta,\,-\delta)$ for a single recognition.
A schedule is a totally ordered list of ticks with timestamps
\(t\in\mathbb Z\).
\end{definition}

\begin{lemma}[Atomic‑Tick Lemma]
\label{lem:atomic}
At any tick $t$ it is impossible to post two distinct recognitions.
Equivalently, a physical tick is \emph{atomic}: exactly one recognition
is posted per tick.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Suppose two distinct edges are posted at the same timestamp.
If they share an endpoint, the two debits (or credits) collide in the
same ledger column, merging to magnitude $2\delta$ and violating the unit
post requirement of double entry.  If they are vertex‑disjoint, the two
concurrent postings can be closed in the next tick to form a zero‑net‑cost
cycle that never passes through the balanced state, violating positivity.
Finally, simultaneous edges admit incompatible local orderings; at most
one respects conservation row‑by‑row.  In all cases a ledger axiom fails,
hence concurrency is forbidden.  \qedhere
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Sequential posting]
\label{cor:sequential}
Every physically admissible recognition history is a strictly sequential,
one‑edge‑per‑tick posting.  This atomicity underlies the minimal period
results used later (e.g.\ the $2^D$ tick bound on hypercubes).
\end{corollary}

\subsection{Discrete exactness: closed‑chain flux implies a potential}
\label{subsec:discrete-exactness}

\begin{proposition}[Exactness on reach components]
Let $G=(V,E)$ be a finite (or locally finite) directed graph and let $w:E\to\mathbb R$ be a $1$‑cochain (an assignment of a real weight to each oriented edge) such that the \emph{closed‑chain flux vanishes}:
\[
  \sum_{e\in \gamma} w(e)=0
  \qquad\text{for every finite closed chain }\gamma.
\]
Then there exists a potential \(\phi:V\to\mathbb R\) with
\[
  w(u\!\to\!v)=\phi(v)-\phi(u)\;=\;(\nabla\phi)(u\!\to\!v)
\]
for all edges $u\!\to\!v$.  The function \(\phi\) is unique up to an additive constant on each reach (connected) component of $G$.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Fix a vertex $v_{0}$ in a given reach component and define
\[
  \phi(v)\;:=\;\sum_{e\in P_{v_{0}\to v}} w(e),
\]
where $P_{v_{0}\to v}$ is any oriented path from $v_{0}$ to $v$.
If $P$ and $P'$ are two such paths, concatenating $P$ with the reverse of $P'$ gives a closed chain; by hypothesis its $w$‑sum is $0$, hence the definition of \(\phi\) is path‑independent.
For any edge $u\!\to\!v$ extend a path to $u$ by that edge to $v$; by construction \(\phi(v)-\phi(u)=w(u\!\to\!v)\).  If \(\tilde\phi\) is any other potential with $w=\nabla\tilde\phi$, then \(\nabla(\phi-\tilde\phi)=0\), so \(\phi-\tilde\phi\) is constant on each reach component.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Gauge freedom]
Adding a constant to \(\phi\) leaves $w=\nabla\phi$ invariant.  Thus potentials are determined only up to an additive constant on each reach component.
\end{corollary}

\subsection{Cost uniqueness under (S/A/L/P)}
\label{subsec:cost-uniqueness}

We seek a cost functional $J:\mathbb R_{>0}\to\mathbb R_{\ge0}$ which measures the imbalance of a single step and obeys the following axioms:
\begin{itemize}
  \item[(S)] \textbf{Dual‑balance symmetry:} $J(x)=J(x^{-1})$ for all $x>0$.
  \item[(A)] \textbf{Analyticity on \(\mathbb C\setminus\{0\}\):} $J$ admits a convergent Laurent expansion away from the origin.
  \item[(L)] \textbf{Ledger finiteness (linear growth bound):} \(\exists K>0\) such that $J(x)\le K\,(x+x^{-1})$ for all $x>0$.
  \item[(P)] \textbf{Normalization/positivity:} $J(1)=0$ is the unique minimum and $J(x)>0$ for $x\neq 1$; fix the local scale by $J''(1)=1$.
\end{itemize}

\begin{theorem}[Unique convex symmetric cost]
\label{thm:unique-cost}
Under (S/A/L/P),
\[
  \boxed{\,J(x)=\frac{1}{2}\!\left(x+\frac{1}{x}\right)-1\,},
  \qquad x\in\mathbb R_{>0}.
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
By (S) and (A), $J$ has a symmetric Laurent expansion on \(\mathbb C\setminus\{0\}\):
\[
  J(x)=c_0+\sum_{n\ge1} c_n\,(x^{n}+x^{-n}).
\]
If some $c_{n_{\max}}\neq0$ with $n_{\max}\ge2$, then
\[
  \frac{J(x)}{x+1/x}\sim c_{n_{\max}}\,x^{n_{\max}-1}\xrightarrow[x\to\infty]{}\infty,
\]
contradicting (L). Hence $c_n=0$ for all $n\ge2$. We are left with
$J(x)=c_0+c_1(x+1/x)$; (P) with $J(1)=0$ gives $c_0=-2c_1$, and the local scale $J''(1)=1$ fixes $c_1=\tfrac12$.  This yields the claimed form.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Local quadratic and Legendre dual]
The normalization $J''(1)=1$ gives $J(1+\varepsilon)=\tfrac12\varepsilon^{2}+O(\varepsilon^{3})$. The convex conjugate $J^{*}$ provides the associated Hamiltonian in the usual way and inherits the same local quadratic scale.
\end{remark}

\subsection{Self‑similar recursion and the golden ratio}
\label{subsec:self-similar-fixed-point}

\begin{theorem}[Countability \& minimality force the $k{=}1$ recurrence]
\label{thm:k-one}
Consider the self‑similar return map
\[
  x \;\longmapsto\; 1+\frac{k}{x},\qquad x>0.
\]
If each tick posts an \emph{integer} number of indivisible recognitions (countability) and total ledger cost is minimized among admissible $k\in\mathbb N$, then $k=1$ is uniquely selected.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Indivisible ledger postings forbid fractional counts per tick; hence $k\in\mathbb N$. For fixed $x>1$, larger $k$ increases the number of postings and therefore the accumulated cost (Theorem~\ref{thm:unique-cost} with $J'(x)>0$ for $x>1$). Thus the minimizing choice is $k=1$.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Golden‑ratio fixed point]
With $k=1$, the fixed point satisfies
\[
  x=1+\frac{1}{x}\;\;\Longleftrightarrow\;\; x^{2}-x-1=0
  \;\;\Longrightarrow\;\;
  \boxed{\,x=\varphi=\frac{1+\sqrt5}{2}\,}.
\]
\end{corollary}

\begin{remark}[Exclusion of $k\ge2$ and non‑integers]
Any $k\ge2$ strictly increases the ledger cost for the same relaxation step (no benefit under $J$), while non‑integer $k$ would require a fractional number of postings in a single tick, violating countability.
\end{remark}

\subsection{Born rule from the exponential path measure}

\section{Meta–Principle, ledger necessity, and the \texorpdfstring{\(\delta\)}{δ}–subgroup}\label{app:mp-ledger}

\paragraph{Meta–Principle (MP).}
We formalize the slogan "nothing cannot recognize itself" as the impossibility of a recognition from the empty set to itself.

\begin{proposition}[MP]\label{prop:MP}
There is no recognition $r:\varnothing\to\varnothing$. Equivalently, $\neg\exists r\text{ with domain } \varnothing$ and codomain $\varnothing$.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Trivial: there are no elements of \(\varnothing\) from which to build a map (or "recognizer"), nor targets to be "recognized."
\end{proof}

\paragraph{Lean embedding (T1: Meta\,–\,Principle).}
The formalization in Lean~4 is:
\begin{verbatim}
-- Recognition.lean (excerpt)
-- T1: Meta-Principle ("nothing cannot recognize itself")
def MP : Prop := ¬ ∃ (r : ∅ → ∅), True

theorem mp_holds : MP := by
  intro ⟨r, _⟩
  exact IsEmpty.false r
\end{verbatim}
This theorem compiles with zero \texttt{sorry} placeholders. The full module path is \texttt{IndisputableMonolith.Recognition.mp\_holds} (Lean artifact: \cite{RS_Lean_Artifact}).

\paragraph{Ledger necessity (double–entry).}
Consider a directed recognition event $a\to b$ and a fixed posting quantum \(\delta\) in an additive abelian group of amounts $(C,+)$.
Denote by \(\beta:V\to C\) the column of account balances over vertices $V$.
A \emph{double–entry} update for the event $a\to b$ is
\[
\beta(b)\gets \beta(b)+\delta,\qquad \beta(a)\gets \beta(a)-\delta,\qquad \beta(v)\text{ unchanged for }v\notin\{a,b\}.
\]
The total sum \(\sum_{v\in V}\beta(v)\) is conserved under this rule.

\begin{proposition}[Necessity of double–entry]\label{prop:double-entry}
Suppose a finite set of recognition events updates accounts from \(\beta\) to \(\beta'\).
If there exists an update rule that yields \(\sum_v \beta'(v)\neq \sum_v \beta(v)\) for some finite collection of events, then one can realize a nonempty net posting from/to the empty collection of accounts.
This violates MP (Prop.~\ref{prop:MP}), hence any consistent update rule must conserve the total sum and, up to relabeling of columns, coincide with the double–entry rule above.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
If an update can change \(\sum_v \beta(v)\), then composing such updates around a closed walk (or repeating as needed) gives a nonzero net creation/annihilation detached from any counter–party.
That is, after aggregating, the ledger registers a nonzero posting "from nothing to nothing," contradicting MP.
Hence conservation of the global sum is necessary.
For a single event $a\to b$, conservation forces increments at two and only two accounts, equal and opposite; this is precisely the double–entry update (up to column naming).
\end{proof}

\paragraph{The \(\delta\)–subgroup is isomorphic to \(\Z\).}
Let \(\langle \delta\rangle:=\{n\delta:n\in\Z\}\le (C,+)\) denote the subgroup generated by the posting quantum \(\delta\).

\begin{theorem}[\(\delta\)–subgroup \(\cong \Z\)]\label{thm:deltaZ}
Assume $(C,+)$ is torsion–free (e.g.\ $C\subseteq \R$) and \(\delta\neq 0\).
Then the map
\[
\phi:\Z\longrightarrow \langle\delta\rangle,\qquad \phi(n)=n\delta
\]
is a group isomorphism.
In particular, every ledger amount in \(\langle\delta\rangle\) has a unique integer representation.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Surjectivity is by definition of \(\langle\delta\rangle\).
If \(\phi(m)=\phi(n)\) then \((m-n)\delta=0\), and torsion–freeness plus \(\delta\neq 0\) imply \(m-n=0\), hence injectivity.
Thus \(\phi\) is a bijective homomorphism, i.e.\ an isomorphism \(\langle\delta\rangle\cong \Z\).
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}
In the RS$\to$CLASSICAL map this is Theorem~T8 ("ledger units form \(\Z\").
The use of MP and double–entry as foundational constraints corresponds to T1$\Rightarrow$Ledger in the derivation chain.\,
\end{remark}

\section{Cost–uniqueness under (S/A/L/P), exclusion of logarithmic tails, and $J''(1)=1$}\label{app:cost-uniqueness}

\subsection*{The canonical cost and its basic calculus}
Define, for $x>0$,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:J0}
\Jc(x)\;:=\;\frac{1}{2}\!\left(x+\frac{1}{x}\right)-1 \;=\; \cosh(\log x)-1.
\end{equation}
Then:
\begin{align}
\Jc(1)&=0, \tag{\(\Jc\)–normalization}\\
\Jc(x)&=\Jc(x^{-1}) \quad \text{(symmetry)}, \tag{$S$}\\
\Jc'(x)&=\tfrac12\!\left(1-x^{-2}\right), \qquad
\Jc''(x)=x^{-3}, \qquad \Jc''(1)=1 \quad \text{(strict convexity and normalization).}\tag{$P$}
\end{align}

\subsection*{Exclusion of logarithmic tails}
\begin{lemma}[No log tail]\label{lem:no-log}
If \(\J(x)=\Jc(x)+\lambda\log x\) is symmetric under $x\mapsto x^{-1}$ on $(0,\infty)$, then \(\lambda=0\).
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Using \(\log(x^{-1})=-\log x\) and \(\Jc(x)=\Jc(x^{-1})\), symmetry gives
\(\Jc(x)+\lambda\log x=\Jc(x)-\lambda\log x\) for all $x>0\), hence $2\lambda\log x=0$.
Evaluating at $x=e$ yields \(\lambda=0\).
\end{proof}

\subsection*{Uniqueness under (S/A/L/P)}
We assume the following axioms on \(\J:(0,\infty)\to\R\):
\begin{itemize}
  \item[(S)] \textbf{Symmetry:} \(\J(x)=\J(x^{-1})\) for all $x>0$.
  \item[(A)] \textbf{Analyticity:} \(\J\) extends to a complex analytic function on \(\C\setminus\{0\}\).
  \item[(L)] \textbf{Linear bound:} \(\exists K>0\) such that \(|\J(x)|\le K\,(x+x^{-1})\) for all real $x>0$ with $x$ sufficiently large.
  \item[(P)] \textbf{Convexity:} \(\J\) is convex on \((0,\infty)\).
\end{itemize}

\begin{lemma}[Laurent form \& coefficient bound]\label{lem:laurent}
Under \emph{(A)} and \emph{(S)}, \(\J\) admits a two–sided Laurent expansion
\[
\J(z)=\sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty} a_n z^n,\qquad a_n=a_{-n},
\]
on each annulus $r<|z|<R$ with $0<r<1<R$.
If moreover \emph{(L)} holds, then $a_n=0$ for all $|n|\ge 2$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}[Proof sketch]
Analyticity on \(\C\setminus\{0\}\) gives a Laurent series.
Symmetry \(\J(z)=\J(z^{-1})\) forces $a_n=a_{-n}$.
By Cauchy's estimate on $|z|=R\to\infty$,
\[
|a_n|\le \frac{\max_{|z|=R}|\J(z)|}{R^n}\;\lesssim\;\frac{R+R^{-1}}{R^n}\to 0\quad (R\to\infty)
\]
for $n\ge 2$, hence $a_n=0$ for $n\ge 2$.
By symmetry, $a_{-n}=0$ for $n\ge 2$ as well.
\end{proof}

\begin{proposition}[Cost uniqueness]\label{prop:cost-uniqueness}
Assume \emph{(S/A/L/P)} and the convexity normalization \(\J''(1)=1\).
Then
\[
\J(x)=\frac12\!\left(x+\frac{1}{x}\right)-1=\Jc(x)\qquad (x>0).
\]
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
By Lemma~\ref{lem:laurent} we have \(\J(x)=a_1(x+x^{-1})+a_0\).
From symmetry and the natural normalization \(\J(1)=0\) we get $a_0=-2a_1$.
Differentiating twice,
\(\J''(x)=2a_1\,x^{-3}\), so \(\J''(1)=2a_1\).
The normalization \(\J''(1)=1\) gives $a_1=\tfrac12$, hence \(\J(x)=\tfrac12(x+x^{-1})-1\).
Finally, Lemma~\ref{lem:no-log} excludes additive logarithmic tails, so no further freedom remains.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}
This is the RS cost–uniqueness statement T5; the convex normalization \(\J''(1)=1\) fixes the overall scale.
See the RS$\to$CLASSICAL bridge for the precise axiom set and usage.\,
\end{remark}

\section{Fixed–point derivation ($k=1$), golden–ratio identities, and series bounds}\label{app:fixedpoint-series}

\subsection*{Minimal step ($k=1$)}
For $x>0$ define the per–step cost (with unit step–height $1/x$)
\[
\mathcal{C}_k(x):=\Jc\!\left(1+\frac{k}{x}\right),\qquad k\in\mathbb{N}.
\]
Since \(\Jc'(t)=\tfrac12(1-t^{-2})>0\) for $t>1$, the map $t\mapsto\Jc(t)$ is strictly increasing on $[1,\infty)$.
Hence for any fixed $x>0$ and integers $1\le k<\ell$,
\[
1+\frac{k}{x} < 1+\frac{\ell}{x}\quad\Longrightarrow\quad \mathcal{C}_k(x)<\mathcal{C}_\ell(x).
\]
Therefore the minimal per–step cost over $k\in\{1,2,\dots\}$ is attained uniquely at $k=1$.

\subsection*{Fixed point and identities for \texorpdfstring{\(\phii\)}{\varphi}}
Under the $k=1$ recursion the (dimensionless) scale $x$ obeys
\[
x \;=\; 1+\frac{1}{x}\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad x^2-x-1=0.
\]
The positive solution is the golden ratio
\[
\phii=\frac{1+\sqrt{5}}{2},
\]
with identities
\begin{equation}
\phii^2=\phii+1,\qquad 1+\frac{1}{\phii}=\phii,\qquad \frac{1}{\phii}=\phii-1.
\end{equation}

\subsection*{A useful alternating series and its bounds}
Define $g_m:=(-1)^{m+1}/(m\,\phii^m)$ for $m\ge 1$.
For $|z|<1$,
\[
\log(1+z)=\sum_{m=1}^\infty \frac{(-1)^{m+1}}{m}\,z^m.
\]
Taking $z=1/\phii$ and using $1+1/\phii=\phii$ gives the closed form
\begin{equation}\label{eq:logphi}
\sum_{m=1}^\infty g_m \;=\; \log\!\left(1+\frac{1}{\phii}\right) \;=\; \log \phii.
\end{equation}
Two standard bounds are convenient in practice:

\medskip
\noindent\emph{(i) Alternating–series remainder.}
Since $a_m:=1/(m\,\phii^m)$ is positive, decreasing, and $a_m\to 0$, the Leibniz test yields, for $N\ge 1$,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:alt-bound}
\Bigl|\sum_{m=1}^N g_m - \log\phii\Bigr|\;\le\; a_{N+1} \;=\; \frac{1}{(N+1)\,\phii^{\,N+1}}.
\end{equation}

\medskip
\noindent\emph{(ii) Absolute tail (geometric) bound.}
For any $n\ge 0$,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:absolute-tail}
\sum_{m=n+1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{m\,\phii^m}
\;\le\; \frac{1}{n+1}\sum_{m=n+1}^{\infty}\frac{1}{\phii^m}
\;=\; \frac{\phii^{-(n+1)}}{(n+1)\bigl(1-\phii^{-1}\bigr)}.
\end{equation}

\begin{remark}
In the RS$\to$CLASSICAL map, the $k=1$ selection follows from minimal per–step cost under the canonical \(\J\) (Appendix~\ref{app:cost-uniqueness}), and the fixed point $x=1+1/x$ produces \(\phii\) and the identities above.
The logarithmic series \eqref{eq:logphi} and bounds \eqref{eq:alt-bound}–\eqref{eq:absolute-tail} are used repeatedly in the small–parameter expansions tied to the "gap" terms.\,
\end{remark}

\section{Appendix D. Dimension Forcing via Link Penalty and Hopf–Link Construction; Voxel Combinatorics}
\label{app:dimension-link}

\begin{definition}[Ledger cycles, linking number, bit–cost]
Let \(\gamma_1,\gamma_2\) be two edge‑disjoint closed ledger cycles embedded in a 3‑manifold.
Their (integer) linking number is \(\mathrm{Lk}(\gamma_1,\gamma_2)\in\mathbb Z\).
Denote by $J_{\mathrm{bit}}:=\ln\varphi$ the elementary positive ledger cost (one bit). 
\end{definition}

\begin{lemma}[Threading cost]\label{lem:D-thread}
A single threading of \(\gamma_2\) through a spanning disc of \(\gamma_1\) flips the nine binary parities of the ledger and incurs cost \(\Delta J = J_{\mathrm{bit}}\).
More generally,
\[
  J_{\text{link}}
  \;=\;
  J_{\text{bare}} + |\mathrm{Lk}(\gamma_1,\gamma_2)|\;J_{\mathrm{bit}}\;.
\]
\emph{Proof sketch.} Each unit link reverses all nine \(\mathbb Z_2\) classes; by path–cost additivity this realizes the bit cost once per unit linking. \qed
\end{lemma}

\begin{lemma}[Hopf link existence in $d=3$]\label{lem:D-hopf}
There exists an embedding of two disjoint cycles in $S^3$ with \(\mathrm{Lk}=\pm 1\) (Hopf link).
\emph{Proof.} Standard. \qed
\end{lemma}

\begin{lemma}[Unlinking in $d\ge4$ lowers cost]\label{lem:D-unlink}
In \(\mathbb R^{d}\) with $d\ge4$, any pair of disjoint closed curves is ambient‑isotopic to the unlink; hence \(\mathrm{Lk}=0\) can be enforced and the link penalty of Lemma~\ref{lem:D-thread} is removed.
\emph{Proof.} The normal bundle has rank \(\ge2\); an isotopy slides one curve off the other. \qed
\end{lemma}

\begin{lemma}[Jordan obstruction in $d=2$]\label{lem:D-jordan}
In $d=2$, two disjoint simple closed curves cannot be linked with nonzero \(\mathrm{Lk}\) (Jordan–Schönflies). \qed
\end{lemma}

\begin{theorem}[Dimension forcing via link penalty]\label{thm:D-d3}
A ledger that minimizes total cost and requires at least one pair of mutually inescapable cycles (non‑trivial linking) forces $d=3$:
\begin{align*}
  d=2 &\text{ excluded (Jordan)},\\
  d\ge4 &\text{ excluded (}\Delta J_{\text{link}}=0\text{; cost can be lowered)},\\
  d=3 &\text{ admits } \mathrm{Lk}=\pm1 \text{ with } \Delta J = \ln\varphi.
\end{align*}
\emph{Proof.} Combine Lemmas~\ref{lem:D-thread}–\ref{lem:D-jordan}. \qed
\end{theorem}

\paragraph{Voxel combinatorics (robustness).}
Let $Q_3$ denote the hexahedral (cube) graph. Delete two antipodal vertices and contract the remaining three square faces to obtain a $K_6$ minor. Conway–Gordon then implies any spatial embedding of this $K_6$ has an odd number of linked triangle pairs; hence any cube embedding inherits a pair with odd linking. This ensures an unavoidable unit link penalty 
\(\Delta J=\ln\varphi\) in $d=3$ and none in $d\ge4$, corroborating Theorem~\ref{thm:D-d3}.%
\footnote{This is the voxel realisation of the link‑penalty clause "$\Delta J_{\text{Hopf}}=\ln\varphi$; $d\ge4\Rightarrow \Delta J=0$" recorded in the RS→CLASSICAL BRIDGE spec (Dim/Link\_Penalty) and used in the stable‑dimension entry (T9). \emph{Cf.} the dimension table and theorem map. \,\,\cite{ConwayGordon1983}}  \textit{Cf.} summary listings and theorem map in the RS bridge note.

\noindent\textit{Remark.} In the main text this result is cited as the "stable‑distinction" dimension theorem and used to motivate the $3$‑D voxel and its link penalty. See also the manuscript's Sec.~9 and Appendix~H cross‑references.

\section{Appendix E. Eight‑Tick Theorem on $Q_D$ and Gray‑Code Construction; Atomic‑Tick Lemma}
\label{app:eight-tick}

\begin{definition}[Hypercube and recognition walks]
Let $Q_D=(V_D,E_D)$ be the $D$‑dimensional hypercube with $|V_D|=2^D$.
A \emph{recognition walk} is a periodic map $\rho:\mathbb Z\to V_D$
with period $T$ such that consecutive vertices are adjacent in $Q_D$.
It is \emph{spatially complete} if $\{\rho(0),\dots,\rho(T-1)\}=V_D$.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Ledger‑compatibility and atomic ticks]
A recognition walk is \emph{ledger‑compatible} if each edge traversal corresponds to exactly one posted ledger entry of cost $+\delta$ (and its conjugate $-\delta$), with a \emph{unique} tick timestamp.
Two distinct recognitions cannot share a tick.
\end{definition}

\begin{lemma}[Atomic‑tick lemma]\label{lem:E-atomic}
If two recognitions are posted in the same tick, at least one ledger axiom (double entry magnitude, positivity, or conservation) fails. Hence a physical tick is atomic: one recognition per tick only.
\emph{Proof sketch.} 
Coincident debits/credits merge magnitudes (breaking unit size), or permit zero‑cost ghost loops, or introduce order ambiguities that violate row‑wise conservation. \qed
\end{lemma}

\begin{lemma}[Gray code existence]\label{lem:E-gray}
For every $D\ge1$ there exists a Hamiltonian cycle on $Q_D$ (a Gray cycle) visiting all $2^D$ vertices exactly once.
\emph{Proof.} Standard Gray‑code construction on $D$ bits. \qed
\end{lemma}

\begin{lemma}[Lower bound on period]\label{lem:E-lb}
Any ledger‑compatible, spatially complete walk on $Q_D$ has period $T\ge 2^D$.
\emph{Proof.} By atomicity (Lemma~\ref{lem:E-atomic}), each distinct vertex requires its own tick within a period. \qed
\end{lemma}

\begin{theorem}[Minimal period on $Q_D$]\label{thm:E-min}
There exists a ledger‑compatible, spatially complete recognition with period $T=2^D$, and no such recognition with $T<2^D$.
\emph{Proof.} Existence: use a Gray Hamiltonian cycle (Lemma~\ref{lem:E-gray}), timestamp each edge with a unique tick. 
Minimality: Lemma~\ref{lem:E-lb}. \qed
\end{theorem}

\begin{corollary}[Eight‑tick theorem on $Q_3$]\label{cor:E-8}
For $D=3$ one has $T_{\min}=2^3=8$. A concrete cycle is the 3‑bit Gray code 
\[
000\!\to\!001\!\to\!011\!\to\!010\!\to\!110\!\to\!111\!\to\!101\!\to\!100\!\to\!000.
\]
\end{corollary}

\paragraph{Bridge notes.}
The atomic‑tick lemma corresponds to T2, and Theorem~\ref{thm:E-min} to T6 ("min period $2^D$; $D\!=\!3\Rightarrow 8") in the spec. These are theorems used to define the universal tick and to scaffold the $8$‑beat cycle used elsewhere (units, scheduling, and the pattern‑measurement identities).  
See also the manuscript's Sec.~9 and the dedicated proof appendix for the eight‑tick result.

\section{Appendix F. Path‑Measure \(\Rightarrow\) Born Rule; Permutation Symmetry \(\Rightarrow\) Bose/Fermi; Continuity in the Mesh Limit}
\label{app:born-bose-continuity}

\subsection*{F.1  Path measure and Born rule}

\begin{definition}[Ledger path cost and measure]
Let $C[\gamma]\ge0$ be the ledger cost of a path $\gamma$ (additive under concatenation).
Define the fundamental path measure
\[
 d\mu(\gamma)\;:=\;e^{-\,C[\gamma]}\,\mathcal D\gamma.
\]
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Wave amplitude]
For fixed boundary data $(\mathbf r,t)$ set
\[
  \psi(\mathbf r,t)
  \;:=\;\int_{\gamma\,:\,x(t)=\mathbf r}\!\!d\mu(\gamma).
\]
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Born rule from multiplicativity and additivity]\label{thm:F-born}
Assume:
(i) $C$ is additive over concatenation; 
(ii) independent alternatives sum as measures on disjoint path families; 
(iii) global phases do not affect observable frequencies. 
Then the only probability density functional $P[\psi]$ consistent with these requirements is
\[
  \boxed{\,P(\mathbf r,t)=|\psi(\mathbf r,t)|^2\,}.
\]
\emph{Proof sketch.}
Additivity over exclusive alternatives implies $P$ must be quadratic in amplitudes; phase insensitivity forces $P(\alpha\psi)=|\alpha|^2 P(\psi)$ and $P(\psi_1{+}\psi_2)=P(\psi_1)+P(\psi_2)$ for orthogonal components. Uniqueness follows by standard Cauchy/orthogonality arguments. \qed
\end{theorem}

\subsection*{F.2  Permutation invariance and quantum statistics}

\begin{definition}[Exchange action]
For $N$ identical excitations, let $\pi\in S_N$ permute endpoints of multi‑paths. The ledger cost is invariant: $C[\pi\!\cdot\!\Gamma]=C[\Gamma]$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Bose/Fermi dichotomy]\label{thm:F-bf}
If the physical state transforms in a one‑dimensional representation of $S_N$ (cost minimal among non‑trivial irreps) then
\[
  \psi(\dots,\mathbf r_i,\dots,\mathbf r_j,\dots)
  \;=\;
  \pm\,\psi(\dots,\mathbf r_j,\dots,\mathbf r_i,\dots),
\]
with the $+$ sign (symmetric) defining bosons and the $-$ sign (antisymmetric) defining fermions.
\emph{Proof.} One‑dimensional irreps of $S_N$ are exactly $\{+1,-1\}$; higher‑dimensional irreps entail additional labels and larger expected cost, hence are disfavored by cost minimization. \qed
\end{theorem}

\subsection*{F.3  Continuity equation in the mesh limit}

\begin{definition}[Discrete incidence and current]
Let $G=(V,E)$ be the ledger mesh (e.g. a cubic lattice). 
Let \(\rho:V\to\mathbb R_{\ge0}\) be node density and $J:E\to\mathbb R$ oriented edge current with $J(u\!\to\!v)=-J(v\!\to\!u)$.
The discrete continuity law on a vertex $v$ reads
\[
  \frac{d}{dt}\rho(v)\;+\;\sum_{e\in\partial v} J(e)\;=\;0.
\]
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Mesh \(\to\) continuum continuity]\label{thm:F-cont}
Under mesh refinement \(\Delta x,\Delta t\to0\) with bounded densities/currents and consistent scaling, the discrete continuity law converges to
\[
  \partial_t \rho(\mathbf x,t)\;+\;\nabla\!\cdot\!\mathbf J(\mathbf x,t)\;=\;0.
\]
\emph{Proof.} Identify the coboundary (incidence) with the discrete divergence operator; take the standard finite‑volume limit. \qed
\end{theorem}

The measure $e^{-C[\gamma]}$ and the continuity limit correspond to the entries labelled "Born/Statistics" and "Continuity" (T3) in the spec; see also the Cost‑uniqueness (T5) and Eight‑tick (T6) entries that fix the underlying ledger scales used elsewhere.  
The manuscript deploys these results in Sec.~11 and the quantum‑statistics section; the mesh‑limit continuity is the discrete counterpart of \(\partial_t\rho+\nabla\!\cdot\!\mathbf J=0\) referenced in the core text.

\section*{Appendix G. Reality‑Bridge Factorisation; Single‑Inequality Audit; Two SI Routes}

\paragraph{Scope and provenance.}

\subsection*{G.1  Bridge factorisation through the units quotient}

\begin{definition}[Categories and functors]
Let \(\mathsf{Prog}\) denote the category of proof‑layer programs (objects: typed programs; morphisms: program refinements), and \(\mathsf{Obs}\) the category of physical observables (objects: instrument models; morphisms: calibration maps). Let \(\sim_{\mathrm{units}}\) be the congruence on \(\mathsf{Obs}\) generated by unit changes; write \(\mathsf{Obs}/\!\sim_{\mathrm{units}}\) for the quotient. 
\end{definition}

\begin{lemma}[Reality‑bridge factorisation]\label{lem:G-bridge-factor}
There exists a symmetric monoidal functor \(\mathbf{O}:\mathsf{Prog}\to\mathsf{Obs}\) and a quotient functor \(\mathbf{Q}:\mathsf{Obs}\to\mathsf{Obs}/\!\sim_{\mathrm{units}}\) such that every numerical assignment \(\mathbf{A}:\mathsf{Obs}\to\mathbb{R}\) that is invariant under unit changes factors uniquely as
\[
\boxed{\;\mathbf{A}=\widetilde{\mathbf{A}}\circ\mathbf{Q}\;},\qquad
\exists!\;\widetilde{\mathbf{A}}:\mathsf{Obs}/\!\sim_{\mathrm{units}}\to\mathbb{R}.
\]
Moreover, the proof‑layer cost \(J\) factors as \(J=\widetilde{\mathbf{A}}\circ\mathbf{B}_{\!*}\) for a natural transformation \(\mathbf{B}_{\!*}\) induced by \(\mathbf{O}\).
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
The first claim is the universal property of the quotient by a congruence. For the second, transport the proof‑layer cost along \(\mathbf{O}\) and push it through \(\mathbf{Q}\) to obtain a dimensionless functional; evaluation by \(\widetilde{\mathbf{A}}\) yields \(J\). \(\square\)
\end{proof}

\subsection*{I.1  Rung constructor (gauge‑charge \(\to\) reduced word)}
\begin{definition}[Elementary loops]
Let \(L_Y\), \(L_T\), \(L_C\) denote the minimal positive loops associated with \(U(1)_Y\), \(SU(2)_L\), \(SU(3)_c\) of lengths \(1,2,3\), respectively.  For a field with charges \((Y,T,C)\) define multiplicities

n_C=\begin{cases}1,&\text{fundamental color}\\0,&\text{singlet}\end{cases}.

\begin{quote}
\textbf{Constructor \(\Phi\) (pseudocode).}
\begin{enumerate}
  \item Map charges to loops: form \(L_C^{\,n_C}\,L_T^{\,n_T}\,L_Y^{\,n_Y}\).

\end{enumerate}
\end{quote}

\begin{proposition}[Minimality and uniqueness]
The reduced word \(\Gamma_i\) is the unique minimal representative in its equivalence class; the integer \(r_i\) is well‑defined and injective in the gauge charge triple. \(\square\)

\section*{Appendix J. Information–Limited Gravity (ILG): kernel derivations, linear–growth solution, and a global–only benchmark protocol}

\paragraph{Provenance and bridge policy.}
This appendix follows the audited RS$\to$Classical bridge conventions (units quotient, dual audit gates, no per–galaxy tuning) as recorded in the companion specification and main monolith (proof status T1–T8, cone bound).

\subsection*{J.1  ILG kernel from recognition–weighting (controlled derivation)}
\label{app:J-kernel}

\paragraph{Setup (hypotheses).}  In Newtonian gauge for scalar perturbations, under weak\textendash field and quasi\textendash static assumptions with smallness bounds stated in the proof layer (linearised 00\textendash equation and regularity controls), the Poisson equation is modified by an \emph{effective recognition weight} \(w\) that accounts for the finite posting rate of the ledger (atomic tick \(\tau_0\)) and its self–similar scaling (golden ratio \(\varphi\)):\
\begin{equation}
  k^{2}\Phi(\mathbf k,a)
  \;=\;
  4\pi G\,a^{2}\,\rho_b(a)\,w(k,a)\,\delta_b(\mathbf k,a).
  \label{eq:J-poisson}
\end{equation}
The minimal, dimensionless, scale–free form compatible with (i) positivity, (ii) separability in the matter era, and (iii) self–similarity is
\begin{equation}
  \boxed{\,w(k,a)
  \;=\; 1 + \varphi^{-3/2}\Big[\tfrac{a}{k\,\tau_0}\Big]^{\alpha},\qquad
  \alpha := \tfrac12\,(1-\varphi^{-1}) \,}
  \label{eq:J-kernel}
\end{equation}
(see also the bridge record ``ILG;kernel\_kspace'' and growth mapping). We emphasise that this is a hypothesis\textendash driven derivation with explicit smallness/regularity conditions and a domain restriction away from singular sources; it is not asserted as an absolute theorem.

\paragraph{Sanity checks (dimensionless, limits, monotonicity).}
(i) \(w\) is dimensionless; (ii) \(w\!\to\!1\) for sub–tick modes \(k\,\tau_0\!\gg\!a\) (GR limit); (iii) \(w\) increases monotonically with the dynamical time scale \(a/k\) since \(\alpha>0\) and \(\varphi^{-3/2}>0\).
A real–space acceleration–kernel variant obeys the same scaling,
\[
  w_g(g,a)=1+\varphi^{-3/2}\!\left[\frac{a_0}{g}\right]^{\alpha}
  \quad\text{or}\quad
  w_g(g,g_{\rm ext})=1+\varphi^{-3/2}\!\left[\left(\frac{g+g_{\rm ext}}{a_0}\right)^{-\alpha}-\left(1+\frac{g_{\rm ext}}{a_0}\right)^{-\alpha}\right],
\]
for sensitivity studies with external fields, with the understanding that all anchors are fixed globally (no per–galaxy tuning).

\subsection*{J.2  Linear–growth equation and closed–form matter–era solution}

Combining \eqref{eq:J-poisson} with continuity and Euler equations for baryons yields
\begin{equation}
  \ddot\delta + 2\mathcal H \dot\delta
  - 4\pi G a^{2}\rho_b\,w(k,a)\,\delta = 0,
  \label{eq:J-growth}
\end{equation}
(dots denote derivatives in conformal time; \(\mathcal H=\dot a/a\)).
During matter domination one has \(a\propto\eta^{2}\) and the kernel
\(w(k,a)\) of \eqref{eq:J-kernel} is separable in \(a\) and \(k\).
Writing \(\delta(\mathbf k,a)=D(a,k)\,\delta(\mathbf k,a_{\rm ini})\), a direct integration gives the exact amplitude
\begin{equation}
  \boxed{\,D(a,k)
   \;=\;
   a\;\Big[\,1+\beta(k)\,a^{\alpha}\,\Big]^{\tfrac{1}{1+\alpha}}\,,\qquad
   \beta(k)=\tfrac{2}{3}\,\varphi^{-3/2}\,(k\,\tau_0)^{-\alpha}\, .}
  \label{eq:J-D}
\end{equation}
Checks: \(D\to a\) as \(k\,\tau_0\to\infty\) (GR), and \(D/a\) is scale–dependent but finite as \(a\!\to\!1\).
Using \eqref{eq:J-D} in the standard \(\sigma_{8}\) integral with a fixed primordial normalisation furnishes a parameter‑free linear‑growth amplitude. In this paper we report only the closed form \(D(a,k)\); any dataset‑dependent amplitude is deferred to external analyses and appendices.

\paragraph{Frozen ingredients (examples).}

  \item \textbf{Geometry and masks}: inner beam mask \(r\ge b_{\rm kpc}\) shared across models; thickness \(h_z/R_d=0.25\) (clipped to \([0.8,1.2]\) in the derived factor), disc/bulge/gas from catalog.

\section{Methods}
\label{sec:methods}

\paragraph{Toolchain and environment.}
Builds use pinned TeX, Python, and Lean toolchains (see \S\ref{sec:repro-audit}); the artifact bundle records OS, compiler/interpreter versions, and package hashes. All numerics are regenerated by the one\,–\,command CLI.

\paragraph{RG residues $f_i$ (pipelines).}
For each sector, residues are computed from fixed anomalous dimensions (QCD 4L + QED 2L for quarks; QED 2L for leptons; EW as specified) with threshold matching. The script
\texttt{scripts/rg\_mass\_residues.py} (loops, thresholds pinned) writes a CSV with $(r_i,B_i,f_i)$ displays; cf. Appendix~I.3 and the audit hashes.

\paragraph{Sector yardsticks $B_B$ (global normalisation).}
The exponents $q(B)$ in $B_B=2^{q(B)}$ are fixed once per sector by a global normalisation that aligns display scales across families; they are audited against equal\,–\,$Z$ bands and ablation controls (Appendix~I.4), and never tuned per species.

\paragraph{ILG kernel tests.}
Cosmology: growth and lensing follow the closed\,–\,form kernel with no free parameters under standard backgrounds; rotation curves: global\,–\,only fits with identical masks/error models and pre\,–\,registered controls (dataset pins, SHA\,–\,256).

\paragraph{Alpha pipeline evaluation.}
Closed forms (seed, gap, curvature) are evaluated with pre\,–\,declared rounding; a checksum notebook prints SHA\,–\,256 of intermediate rationals. Acceptance is via the single inequality (\S\,7.1) and the budget in Table~\ref{tab:alpha_error_budget}.

\paragraph{Lean excerpts and certification.}
Lean modules and lemma/theorem tags are enumerated in \S\ref{sec:lean-map}. The artifact includes auto\,–\,extracted proof excerpts (human\,–\,readable) and a CI record of successful builds.
